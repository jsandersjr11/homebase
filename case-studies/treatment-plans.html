Kareo Treatment Plans

Overview

About the project
Mental health providers offer services to support an individual's mental health and to help treat mental and behavioral disorders. One of the tools providers use to effectively treat patients and track progress is a treatment plan. The purpose of a treatment plan is to help guide a patient towards reaching goals and help providers to monitor progress and make treatment adjustments when necessary. The treatment plan is a detailed plan tailored to the individual patient and is a powerful tool for engaging the patient in their treatment.
My Role
UX Designer
Timeline
3 months
Team Members
I worked closely with a product manager, and engineering lead, and three engineers. My cross-functional partners included members from Sales , Customer Onboarding, Support, and Training.

Understand

Going out, seeing for myself, and listening to others provides empathy and context around people and their experiences.
Discovery Research
When developing a research plan, the first thing I always ask my team is “What is it that we want to learn?” I document all our questions as well as assumptions that need to be validated. Based on this list, I evaluate the most appropriate research methods to help us answer the questions we write down.

Based on the questions we had, we did the following:
- Conducted 7 customer interviews to understand their current treatment planning process
- Sent a survey to help us understand needs when it came to documenting treatment
- Met with Sales reps to understand what prospect customers’ asks

Define

The right measurable outcome is based on understanding the needs, perceptions, and experiences of those we are attempting to serve.
User Problem
Because of a lack of treatment plan note type / template providers don't have the proper tools to build a structured and coherent treatment plan or an easy way to review a it during the patient visit. During a typical visit therapists will document progress / adjust it if necessary to satisfy the demands of HMOs, managed care companies, third-party payers, and state and federal agencies. Because of the lack of Treatment Plans providers and patients also don't have the ability to electronically sign a it or get alerted when it's time review on update the treatment plan.
Business Problem
One of the top sales blockers in Mental Health includes the lack of treatment plan functionality in Kareo. Treatment plans are essential for Mental Health providers to providing well-rounded care by helping them build a clear, actionable plan for patients that can be monitored during treatment.

Explore

Multiple directions are explored, building on one another, to get to unique and desirable solutions.
Team Ideation activity
As we talk through different themes and trends, we used bright colored stickies for our design ideas. I like to encourage everyone to put their ideas up, no matter how crazy they are!
Prototyping through iteration
Iterating by creating multiple prototype versions was essential to making sure we got the treatment plans feature right due to the complexity of some patients treatment and the time commitment from our engineers.

Create

Testing an idea with minimal resources will lead to a better understanding of the problem and the impact your solutions might have more quickly.

My Design Process

Learn
Synthesize
Ideate
Prototype
Test
Iterate
Learn

Learning is first and foremost about gaining empathy for the user. But, learning is also much more than that. Each project brings new challenges and new domains. Whether the domain is health, education, or business planning, there is always a need to learn.

Synthesize

It’s time to take everything that I learned and use it to identify trends, create and/or identify user’s mental models, user personas, user scenarios, user flows, problem statements, and design principles. Post-it notes, markers, and whiteboards are my best friends here.

Ideate

Ideation is perhaps my favorite part of the process because I (initially) get to let my imagination run wild with creating user scenarios, sketching wireframes, laying out interfaces in Sketch and using any other method I might need to help me in finding solutions to the problems uncovered in the previous phases.

Prototype

With a lot of ideas generated it is time to take the best ideas and turn them into something that can be tested with users. I find it best to learn earlier rather than later what ideas are working and worth pursuing so my prototypes are usually low to mid-fidelity. Though I enjoy getting to make higher-fidelity prototypes that involve gestures and animations whenever I can.

Test

Testing is something that should be done throughout the process (instead of only at the end) in both formal and informal ways, the goal always being to receive feedback on the work being done and to gain an understanding of the thought process others go through when using the product.

Iterate

It is pretty rare that you get everything right the first time around. Having learned from testing a prototype with users I can now refine my solution to better solve the problems that I am trying to solve.
