Church & State

Overview

What is Church & State?
Church & State is a no-strings-attached business incubator for the community to use and build.
My Role
Lead UX Designer

Understand

Going out, seeing for myself, and listening to others provides empathy and context around people and their experiences.
Interviews
I conducted stakeholder interviews with key members of the Church & State board of directors in order to gain a deeper understanding of their needs and challenges. I also spoke with Church & State members to try and uncover hidden challenges that they are facing as members of Church & State.
Online Surveys
Taking the insight I received from my interviews I developed a survey with very precise questions to send out to all Church & State members and tenants. The goal of this survey was to create a baseline for tenant satisfaction as well as gain valuable feedback on their experience with Church & State.

Define

The right measurable outcome is based on understanding the needs, perceptions, and experiences of those we are attempting to serve.
Problem Statement
Church & State is a new unique coworking space that is looking to find a way to increase membership. The unique space offers a breathtaking atmosphere to get your work done but currently lacks in members. Coworking is a new concept to SLC and needs to find a better way to market itself to the general public as a unique coworking space and gain membership. Church & State is non-profit so it has some constraints to spending.
Research and findings document

Explore

Multiple directions are explored, building on one another, to get to unique and desirable solutions.
Wireframes
I created wireframes to connect the visual design of the Church & State site to the information architecture. The process of wireframing helped me to uncover different methods for representing or displaying different types of content and information as well as prioritizing that content in order of importance to the user, their expectations, and their goals.

My Design Process

Learn
Synthesize
Ideate
Prototype
Test
Iterate
Learn

Learning is first and foremost about gaining empathy for the user. But, learning is also much more than that. Each project brings new challenges and new domains. Whether the domain is health, education, or business planning, there is always a need to learn.

Synthesize

It’s time to take everything that I learned and use it to identify trends, create and/or identify user’s mental models, user personas, user scenarios, user flows, problem statements, and design principles. Post-it notes, markers, and whiteboards are my best friends here.

Ideate

Ideation is perhaps my favorite part of the process because I (initially) get to let my imagination run wild with creating user scenarios, sketching wireframes, laying out interfaces in Sketch and using any other method I might need to help me in finding solutions to the problems uncovered in the previous phases.

Prototype

With a lot of ideas generated it is time to take the best ideas and turn them into something that can be tested with users. I find it best to learn earlier rather than later what ideas are working and worth pursuing so my prototypes are usually low to mid-fidelity. Though I enjoy getting to make higher-fidelity prototypes that involve gestures and animations whenever I can.

Test

Testing is something that should be done throughout the process (instead of only at the end) in both formal and informal ways, the goal always being to receive feedback on the work being done and to gain an understanding of the thought process others go through when using the product.

Iterate

It is pretty rare that you get everything right the first time around. Having learned from testing a prototype with users I can now refine my solution to better solve the problems that I am trying to solve.
